Protection System Details

How Verification Protects All Parties​

This page explains exactly how our verification system works to protect investors, projects, and influencers from fraud.

Step-by-Step Verification Process​

Phase 1: Account Connection​

What Happens: Influencer initiates connection to their social media accounts through standard authentication flows.

User Experience:

  1. Click "Verify Account" button
  2. Redirected to Twitter/Instagram/TikTok/YouTube
  3. Platform asks "Allow access to analytics?"
  4. Influencer grants permission
  5. Return to our platform

Security:

  • We never see influencer's password
  • Standard platform authentication (same as any app)
  • Influencer can revoke access anytime
  • Read-only access (we can't post/edit)

Why Only Account Owners Can Do This: Only the real account owner can grant analytics access. Scammers with screenshots can't pass this step.

Phase 2: Data Collection​

What We Pull:

From Twitter:

  • Total follower count
  • Follower growth over time (not just current)
  • Tweet impressions and engagements
  • Audience demographics (locations, interests)
  • Real engagement rates (likes, retweets, replies)

From Instagram:

  • Follower count and growth patterns
  • Post reach and engagement
  • Story views and interactions
  • Audience demographics
  • Saved posts and shares

From TikTok:

  • Follower count and growth
  • Video views and engagement
  • Audience demographics
  • Share and comment patterns

From YouTube:

  • Subscriber count and growth
  • Video views and watch time
  • Engagement rates (likes, comments)
  • Audience retention
  • Demographics and locations

What We Don't Pull:

  • Private messages
  • Contact information
  • Personal data beyond public analytics
  • Anything not relevant to verification

Phase 3: Fraud Pattern Analysis​

What We Check:

1. Follower Authenticity

  • Sudden spikes (bot purchases show as instant jumps)
  • Organic growth patterns (gradual, consistent increase)
  • Follower account quality (real profiles vs empty accounts)
  • Follower activity levels (active users vs dormant bots)

Example Red Flag: Account gains 50,000 followers overnight = likely bot purchase

2. Engagement Patterns

  • Engagement clustering (all activity at same time = coordinated bots)
  • Natural distribution (activity throughout day/week = real humans)
  • Engagement quality (meaningful comments vs generic "great post!")
  • Engagement to follower ratio (realistic vs inflated)

Example Red Flag: 100K followers but only 50 likes per post = fake followers

3. Audience Demographics

  • Relevant to crypto (tech-interested, investment-focused)
  • Geographic distribution (natural spread vs all from one region)
  • Age and interest alignment with crypto market
  • Language and engagement patterns

Example Red Flag: Crypto influencer with 80% followers interested in baby products = bought followers

4. Historical Performance

  • Previous campaign results (if available)
  • Long-term engagement trends
  • Account age and activity history
  • Pattern of deleted/hidden content

Example Red Flag: Account created recently with massive following = likely purchased

Phase 4: Verification Decision​

Pass Verification:

  • Real followers confirmed
  • Natural engagement patterns
  • Relevant audience for crypto
  • No fraud indicators detected
  • Historical performance checks out

Result: Green verification badge, can accept campaigns

Fail Verification:

  • Bot purchases detected
  • Coordinated engagement patterns
  • Irrelevant audience demographics
  • Multiple fraud indicators
  • Suspicious historical patterns

Result: Cannot accept campaigns until issues resolved

Under Review:

  • Some concerns but not definitive
  • Need additional data
  • Manual review by team
  • Influencer can provide explanation

How This Protects Each Party​

Protection for Investors​

Before Platform:

  • No way to verify influencer authenticity
  • Can't see if project hired scammers
  • Investment decisions based on false information
  • No transparency into marketing spend

With Platform:

  • See which influencers promoted project
  • Check if influencers are verified
  • View influencer's full history
  • Make informed investment decisions
  • Avoid projects using unverified promoters

Example: Investor researching new token sees it was promoted by influencer with verified 500K real followers and successful track record → More confidence in project legitimacy.

Protection for Projects​

Before Platform:

  • Can't verify follower counts (screenshots fake-able)
  • Pay for bot networks unknowingly
  • Waste money on fake engagement
  • No recourse if influencer doesn't deliver

With Platform:

  • Only hire verified influencers
  • Real follower counts confirmed
  • Authentic engagement guaranteed
  • Payment protected until delivery
  • Build reputation for hiring quality

Example: Project wants to hire influencer claiming 1M followers. Verification reveals only 200K real followers. Project avoids wasting money on inflated numbers.

Protection for Influencers​

Before Platform:

  • Lumped in with scammers
  • Hard to prove authenticity
  • Lose clients to fake competition
  • Payment disputes common

With Platform:

  • Verification proves authenticity
  • Differentiate from scammers
  • Command premium rates
  • Guaranteed payments
  • Build verifiable reputation

Example: Influencer with real 50K followers competes with scammer claiming 500K fake followers. Verification shows truth. Real influencer wins client.

Permanent Record System​

What Gets Recorded​

Every Campaign:

  • Project name and blockchain
  • Influencer identity (verified)
  • Campaign deliverables specified
  • Content links published
  • Payment amounts
  • Completion timestamps
  • Both parties' ratings
  • Dispute outcomes (if any)

Where It's Recorded​

On Blockchain:

  • Permanent, can't be edited
  • Publicly viewable
  • Searchable by anyone
  • Timestamped forever
  • Verifiable by all

Why Blockchain:

  • Can't delete failed campaigns
  • Can't hide scam attempts
  • Can't edit history
  • Transparent for everyone
  • Truly permanent accountability

How to Use Reputation Data​

For Investors: Search influencer name → See all campaigns promoted → Check project outcomes → Identify patterns

For Projects: Search influencer name → See track record → Check completion rates → Hire confidently

For Influencers: Build reputation over time → Show successful campaigns → Prove professionalism → Command higher rates

Fraud Prevention Mechanisms​

What We Prevent​

1. Fake Follower Purchases How: Direct API access shows real follower counts, growth patterns reveal sudden purchases

2. Bot Engagement Networks How: Engagement pattern analysis detects coordinated activity, clustering

3. Manipulated Screenshots How: Don't accept screenshots, only direct platform data

4. Inflated Reach Claims How: Verify actual reach through impression data, not self-reported numbers

5. Hidden Scam History How: Permanent on-chain records can't be deleted or hidden

6. Rebranding After Scams How: Verification tied to account identity, history follows influencer

Dispute Resolution​

When Disputes Occur​

Project Claims:

  • Content doesn't match agreement
  • Quality below specified standards
  • Deliverables not completed
  • Timeline not met

Influencer Claims:

  • Project changed requirements
  • Payment amount disputed
  • Unfair rejection of work
  • Project unresponsive

Resolution Process​

Step 1: Evidence Collection

  • Original campaign agreement reviewed
  • Submitted content examined
  • Communication logs checked
  • Timestamps verified

Step 2: Review Period

  • Both parties submit evidence
  • Platform reviews all materials
  • Third-party review if needed
  • Objective criteria applied

Step 3: Decision

  • Clear violation → Funds released to appropriate party
  • Ambiguous case → Split resolution or re-work
  • Intentional fraud → Penalties applied + reputation impact

Step 4: Permanent Record

  • Dispute outcome recorded on-chain
  • Both parties' reputations updated
  • Evidence preserved
  • Searchable for future reference
