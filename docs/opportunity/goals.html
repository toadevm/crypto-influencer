Our Goals

We're at the concept stage. These are goals we're aiming for, not guarantees or promises.

Most startups fail. Execution is hard. Markets are unpredictable.

Short-Term Goals (First 6 Months)​

Platform Development:

  • Launch basic marketplace
  • Verification system working
  • Payment automation functional
  • Core features operational

User Onboarding:

  • 50-100 verified influencers
  • 20-30 projects willing to test
  • Mix of small and mid-size campaigns
  • Diverse blockchain representation

Campaign Volume:

  • 50-100 completed campaigns
  • Average $3K-5K per campaign
  • Both parties satisfied
  • Repeat customers

Learning:

  • User feedback collected
  • Pain points identified
  • Feature priorities validated
  • Business model tested

Challenges:

  • Influencers may not see value in verification
  • Projects might prefer status quo (Discord/Telegram)
  • Technical challenges could delay launch
  • Market conditions might worsen
  • Execution could be harder than expected

How we'll respond:

  • Listen to user feedback
  • Pivot if model doesn't work
  • Address challenges directly
  • Adjust goals based on reality

Success metric: Validation that people want this, not revenue targets.

Medium-Term Goals (6-12 Months)​

If Initial Validation Succeeds​

Platform Evolution:

  • Self-serve campaign creation
  • Automated matching algorithm
  • Enhanced analytics dashboard
  • Mobile-friendly interface

User Growth:

  • 200-300 active influencers
  • 50-100 paying projects
  • 500+ campaigns completed
  • Multi-chain support proven

Business Metrics:

  • Consistent month-over-month growth
  • Positive word-of-mouth
  • Repeat campaign rate >50%
  • Clear product-market fit signals

Financial:

  • Approaching break-even
  • Unit economics validated
  • Sustainable cost structure
  • Path to profitability clear

Challenges:

  • Initial validation might fail
  • Competition could emerge
  • Regulatory changes might occur
  • Market could turn down
  • Technical limitations might arise

How we'll respond:

  • Continuously gather feedback
  • Adapt strategy as needed
  • Consider pivot if necessary
  • Maintain financial discipline

Success metric: Clear evidence of product-market fit, not specific numbers.

Long-Term Vision (Year 2+)​

If Product-Market Fit is Achieved​

Market Position:

  • Recognized as "the verified platform"
  • Industry standard for influencer verification
  • Network effects working (more users = more value)
  • Sustainable competitive advantage

Platform Maturity:

  • Comprehensive feature set
  • Excellent user experience
  • High reliability
  • Strong reputation

Business Sustainability:

  • Consistently profitable
  • Multiple revenue streams
  • Healthy gross margins
  • Lean operations

Ecosystem Development:

  • Consider native token utility
  • Partner integrations
  • Additional services
  • Expanded use cases

Challenges:

  • Might never achieve product-market fit
  • Competitive pressure could intensify
  • Market might evolve differently
  • Resource constraints could limit growth
  • Unforeseen challenges could arise

How we'll respond:

  • Stay focused on core value
  • Maintain financial discipline
  • Be willing to pivot or stop
  • Keep communicating transparently

Success metric: Sustainable, growing business solving real problem.

What We're NOT Promising​

No Guarantees On:​

❌ Specific revenue targets ❌ User growth numbers ❌ Market share percentages ❌ Valuation milestones ❌ Token launch timelines ❌ Exit opportunities

Why: The future is unpredictable.

What We Commit To:​

✅ Transparent communication ✅ Transparent reporting ✅ Listening to users ✅ Adapting to reality ✅ Financial discipline ✅ Ethical operation

Potential Challenges​

Scenario 1: No Market Demand​

What it looks like:

  • Influencers don't want verification
  • Projects prefer informal channels
  • Value proposition doesn't resonate

What we'd do:

  • Acknowledge failure quickly
  • Learn from experience
  • Consider pivot or shutdown
  • Transparent communication throughout

Scenario 2: Can't Execute​

What it looks like:

  • Technical challenges insurmountable
  • Can't build features needed
  • Operations too complex
  • Costs exceed revenue potential

What we'd do:

  • Honest assessment of capabilities
  • Seek help if fixable
  • Shutdown if not viable
  • Return any unused funds if possible

Scenario 3: Competition Wins​

What it looks like:

  • Larger player enters market
  • Better execution by competitor
  • Can't differentiate

What we'd do:

  • Focus on niche advantages
  • Consider partnership or acquisition
  • Graceful exit if necessary
  • Learn from experience

Success Scenarios​

Scenario 1: Modest Success​

What it looks like:

  • Sustainable niche business
  • 100-200 campaigns/month
  • Break-even to modest profit
  • Small but loyal user base

Outcome: Decent outcome for early investors, solves problem for users

Scenario 2: Strong Growth​

What it looks like:

  • Clear market leader
  • 500-1,000+ campaigns/month
  • Strong profitability
  • Network effects evident

Outcome: Excellent outcome for investors, significant impact on industry

Scenario 3: Market Transformation​

What it looks like:

  • Industry standard platform
  • Thousands of campaigns monthly
  • Multiple revenue streams
  • Ecosystem around platform

Outcome: Exceptional outcome, but least likely (most startups don't achieve this)

  • What's not working
  • Problems encountered
  • Lessons learned
  • Adjustments made

Both successes and failures shared publicly.

Our Commitment​

What We Promise​

We will:

  • Work hard to validate the model
  • Listen to users constantly
  • Adapt based on feedback
  • Communicate transparently always
  • Fail fast if model doesn't work
  • Celebrate success if it does

We won't:

  • Pretend to know the future
  • Make promises we can't keep
  • Hide problems or challenges
  • Waste investor money
  • Continue if model clearly broken
  • Mislead about progress

Questions to Ask Us​

Healthy Skepticism Welcome​

Good questions investors should ask:

  • "What's your biggest concern about this model?"
  • "What would make you shut this down?"
  • "How will you know if it's working?"
  • "What's your burn rate plan?"
  • "When will you know about product-market fit?"
  • "What's the most likely failure mode?"

We encourage critical thinking. If you're not somewhat skeptical, you're not paying attention.
